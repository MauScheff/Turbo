I'm reading through the /pricing page... and a few things come up... also as constructive feedback, to whomever managed that page, I think it can be made more clear / organized (the info itself)... don't really know who is best to share this but you can forward it if you want... (I was a product manager, I'm also a self-taught designer, I've been a CTO making purchase decisions, I'm an engineer, and I'm an entrepreneur trying to sell my own products, so coming from that perspective)...

And if you asked me as customer how I want to pay, I would maybe have a better "ideal" model (not necessarily pay less but structured differently)... for example:
- Everyone starts as a hobbyist and wants to get started fast, I would put the free unison cloud up front above the fold and first.
- People want to compare everything in one place, it seems like two offers now: first the BYOC and then last the Unison Cloud. Maybe put them all in the same row?
- Also it seems like Unison Cloud Managed is an "add-on", I would put it as a 5th plan option in line with the other possibilities.
- What's a little hard is like sometimes people like me you want to start selling something and see if it works and very often it doesn't but you get to experience unison cloud and get excited and tell others about it.
- If I were following the pricing page strictly, the moment I try to sell something, I already have to pay $110 per month (and manage my own infrastructure), so there's this gap between I'm experimenting with Unison and I'm in business and happy to pay, were I think it could be better covered by Unison (more like a friendly ladder)...
- I'm unable to answer some basic questions as someone not too familiar with Unison, quickly when scanning, like "How much will this acutally cost me?" I don't know in the BYOC which seems to be the only case technically speaking how many nodes will I need, what's a node, etc...
- $15 per user is expensive (eg. compare with github / gmail for work / notion etc...)
- $15 per service is expensive (even paying per service I think is) and may not feel "fair" to some, I don't need more than one service probably, so I'm speaking as a hypthetical enteprise user, and if I had, for anything non-critical I may as well run it on another cloud, like I'm doing with the notifications worker in cloudflare workers...  paying per service doesn't really feel like a good deal... I think people rather think of usage... eg. I/O, memory, etc... in their mental model but if it's their own cluster I guess you wouldn't have visibility on that... but in general I don't like this.
- The other jump is also a bit high so basically the way I'm reading it is:
    - If you're starting out, it's free, but you need to self-report when you're using it commercially, and this info is at the bottom of the page, most people seeing this page first time will look for this info first.
    - then, if you want to hook your own domain and run a small project (what I'm doing) you pay 10 per month to run on the public cloud (also not that clear what public cloud means). This is good! Show this Above the fold! everyone will go through this first I guess. Would be good to be able to be commercial (and here) while you're small... you could say number of requests or something to estimate size....
    - Then if you are commercial you have to pay minimum 110 and go through the pain of setting up your own cluster in AWS (not clear that is AWS only for now), doesn't matter if you're small, or just starting out... this is already a reason to abandon for many small ones (eg. can I afford $1,200 per year to run my small business  on unison cloud vs pay-as-you-go on Firebase / other cloud and spend maybe $50 ...?)
- $ 80 per node also sounds high, specially when you're not clear what that means and how many you will need... but it's reasonable, like I just checked and I probably can go very far with 1 node only in my use case... but when I first saw that it seemed beyond my reach...
- You're also paying for infrastructure on the other cloud, so this is basically like software licensing cost... I think some people prefer OSS for this reason, and during my Unison meetup with other people where I talked about Unison, most people were kind of turned off that it's kind of vendor lock-in and that basically "if you use Unison you have to pay to Unison” as compared to the mental model that most languages / OS and other software you can use for free and do whatever you want with it, and sometimes there's an enterprise version but it's optional...
- The other jump from 0 -> 110 -> minimum 1000 per month was also a bit high, like ideally I would love to not worry about infrastructure in my own cluster, and be able to "just deploy" and pay as a business (based on how much I'm using / can afford) for managed infrastructure, like I feel I can do on other cloud providers... often some SaaS has in their pricing page 3 package tiers and the third one is often like "Let's talk" (when you're a a giant).
- In general it feels a little convoluted the info, and I don't know what the right model is, because eg. if as a growth strategy Unison caters to businesses first, that would make sense to highlight the clusters on top... but it still reads a bit complicated...

Sharing this as the perspective of a customer who loves Unison and wants it to grow and grow with it. Hope this helps!
